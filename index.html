Dragon Age Inquistion is the third installment of BioWare's Dragon Age Series continuing the Journey, introduced in Dragon Age Origins and Dragon Age 2, throughout the continent of Thedas.

Annoucement and Timeline

May 19th, 2011: Game annouced via twitter

September 17th 2012: The title is formally confirmed as Dragon Age III: Inquisition

E3 Press Conference 2013: EA annouces the game is due for release in autumn of 2014

April 22nd 2014: trailer released confirming an October 7th 2014 release date

July 22nd 2014: release delayed to november 18th for North America and November 21st for Europe

November 18th 2014: title released in North America for Playstation 3, Playstation 4, Xbox 360, Xbox one, and PC

November 21st 2014: title released in Europe for Playstation 3, Playstation 4, Xbox 360, Xbox one, and PC

2014 - 2015: BioWare continues to win multiple awards such as IGN'S Game Of The Year, SXSW Game Of The Year, GLAAD Media Award for Special Recognition, and more

Development

Development of Dragon Age Inquisition began prior to the release of Dragon Age II. It was proginally supposed to be a multiplayer-only game, codenamed "Blackfoot". The game differs somewhat from it's predecessors, introducing new features and combining elements of earlier installments in the series into the game.

BioWare uses the new DICE frostbite 3 engine

Combat focuses more ona player's ability to prepare, position and form a cohesive team with their party members, requiring more tactical thinking. The armor customization also differs from it's predecessors as each party member has interchangeable armor pieces that customize the look according to its specification and class.

The protagonist of this installment is known as the Inquisitor. Players are able to choose from multiple races, and the game recognizes game flags, class flags, or a combination of the two depending on the situation. The advent of multiple player races by necessity requires different body and armor models for the protagonist.

Developers Mike Laidlaw and Cameron Lee stated that the game is a total of 150 hours of gameplay. With 50 hours of the main quest, and 100 hours of side quests
